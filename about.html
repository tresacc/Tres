This site is intended to serve Lake Conroe Drive homeowners by providing public information regarding deeds and restrictions and general information about Architectural Control Committee activities.

A Brief History of Lake Conroe Drive Development

(Bill and Agnes D’Amico - Developers)

1943: M.J. and Mayme Ross purchased 363 Acres in Montgomery County, Elijah Collard for about $19K (#47521). This land extended from what is now League Line Road to FM830 in Willis.

1972-3:  D’Amico purchases three sets of land.

Bill and Agnes D’Amico purchased three sets of land from the MJ Ross Estate:

  • 5.935 Acres (#293461 on 7/26/1972) The land currently at the end of Lake Conroe Drive for $42K
  • 17.5 Acres (#3115670 on 2/26/1973) – for $103K – This is what is now referred to as Reserve B, and C.
  • 46.85 Acres (#314001 on 3/26/1973) Combines with the 17.5 acres to form what is now Tres Subdivision – for $284K

1975 -    Phase 1: 5.93 Acre Deed Restrictions, ACC and Sales

  • D’Amico creates a Deed Restriction Document for the 5.93 Acres which includes the formation of an Architectural Control Committee. Much of the wording of the restrictions would be carried to all the deeds used on Lake Conroe Drive. (#7414020 – 7/11/1975)
  • D’Amico files an Easement and access document protecting access across the Lake Conroe Drive properties (#7514994 - 7/24/1975)
  • Sales:
    • 1975 - D’Amico sells a .39 AC parcel of the 5.93 acres to Timothy and Judith Eby (#7514996 7/24/1975 .39 AC)
    • 1978 – D’Amico Sells 3 lots to Maddux Construction (.519 - #7811999, .63 - #781200, .51 - #7812392) with apparently builds and sells to Williams, Carmichael, and Dave Layton.
    • 1983 – D’Amico sells lot to Coffer and lot to Beulah D’Amico (#8723473)
    • 1984 – D’Amico pays off land to Ross family and secures full ownership (#8420191)
    • 1987 – D’Amico grants deed to Coffer (#8726502)

1988 – Lake Conroe Land Cleared of Trees (#8833789)

1990 – Land in 46.85 begins to be sold (#9039237 - D'Amico - Winfred Lee  - 7 Acres) with deed restrictions and the ACC specified. This language appears in every deed of the soon to be platted Tres Subdivision. In the following year, D’Amico sold an additional 2 Acres to Mr. Lee (#911185) and includes his deed restrictions.

1991 - 5.93 Street Access and Maintenance Agreement is signed by the 6 owners of the 5.93 lots (#9131104)


Phase 2-3 : 63 Acre Development and Sales

  • On March 30, 1992, Bill D’Amico platted and created “Tres: A Subdivision containing 63.611 Acres of land in the Elijah Collard Survey, A-7, Montgomery County, Texas. 18 Lots, 2 Blocks, 3 Reserves” (#9214472).
  • D’Amico first gathered and filed consent from existing land owners (Hensen #9302011, Wale #9302012, and Lee #9302013)
  • D’Amico then proceeded to sell land lots for building and included with each sale the same used in 1990. With each sale, Bill D’Amico included the afore mentioned deed restrictions. (Ref: #9303482, #9327837, #9342544, #9402176, etc.)

 

1993:  5.93 Lakefront Access Created and Sold with Restrictions and D'Amico ACC oversight

  • Joe Carmichael (#9337239 - .41ac) for 18K (restrictions for boat house added)
  • Walter Coffer (#9337241 - .24ac) for 5.5K. (restrictions for boat house added)
  • These becomes the second type of restriction document that would be in three of the deeds with oversight authority specified as "D’Amico, their heirs or their assigns".

2004: Bill D’Amico Jr, a licensed realtor in Texas, begins serving as Agent and Attorney in Fact for Bill Sr. (#2004122544, #2009006747)

2007:   Bill and Agnes D’Amico sell their home to Don Schrock with Restrictions that are applied to Reserve C lots (#2007037658).

2007:   Melanie Faulkner (with her brother Bill D’Amico Jr.) sells remaining property in 5.93 (Combined two lots #2002004881 and # 2007030473 into one # 2007030474) with restrictions that come from both Reserve B (Bitgood - # 2003033613) and Coffer/ Carmichael – for lakefront (#9337239).

2008: Bill D’Amico dies 9-28-2008 with Bill D’Amico Jr. acts as the power of attorney for the Subdivision.

2012-2017: Building activity is relatively quite on Lake Conroe Drive – as most lots are developed.

2018-2020: Some construction activity (and violation issues) increase on Lake Conroe Drive.

Deed Restrictions (Four types used along Lake Conroe Drive):

1)   5.93 Acre Deed Restrictions:

    • Deed Restrictions were first applied for the 6 lots that comprised the 5.93 AC portion of land. One restriction was applied to all properties on 7-11-1975 (#7514020).
    • Two weeks later, the sale of the first property was made to Timothy and Judith Eby (#7514996 7/24/1975 .39 AC). It that deed, the convents and restrictions are mentioned, but were not specifically included in the deeds. (This would change on all subsequent deeds.)

2)  5.93 Lakefront Deed Restrictions:

    • In 1993, D’Amico divided the lakefront property to the north of his home into three separate lots. For these lots he created a separate set of Restrictions that pertains to building boat houses
    • He sold two at the same time. One to Carmical (#9337239) one to Coffer (#9337241).
    • In 2007, Bill D’Amico Jr. combined the third lakefront piece with the Faulkner land (#2007030473) and then, at the same time, sold the new combined land to Hitchcock (#2007030474) – for which sale Bill Jr. added restrictions that come from both Reserve B (Bitgood - # 2003033613) and Coffer and Carmichael – lakefront (#9337239).

3)  46 Acre Deed Restrictions:

    • In September 1990, 18 months before the TRES Subdivision plat, D'Amico sold a 7 Acre portion of this land to Winfred Lee (#9039237 - D'Amico - Winfred Lee - 7 Acres) and later a 2 Acre portion to Mr. Lee (#911185) and to Michael Wale on 9-25-1991 (#9144334).
    • In each sale, D’Amico used the same restrictions and pointing to the D’Amico ACC as the approving authority for all building.
    • In 1992, D’Amico secured approval from the owners to create the Tres Subdivision. (Hensen #9302011, Wale #9302012, and Lee #9302013)
    • Between 1992-1999, D’Amico used the same Restrictions for all lot sales with little or no alteration.

4)  17.5 Acre Deed Restrictions:

    1. In 1997, D’Amico began selling portions of the 17.5 Acres - named Reserve A, B, C.
    2. While much of the verbiage remains the same as those are some variations for 10 properties that make up this section of the Subdivision. It is important to reference directly to each deed for this area – as they vary slightly – and some include the helicopter restriction.
    3. In April 2007, D’Amico sold three tracks to Don Schrock which formed his property. Tract1 with his house, Track 2 with the waterfront, and a portion of Reserve C (South of the house). (#200703765 ). D’Amico also included the restrictions common to the 17.5 Acres – including the helicopter restrictions. And added that any replanting of the three tracks must be done with the approval of the neighbors.
